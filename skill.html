How to Patch a Large Hole in Drywall

Whether you're a home owner, a renter, have kids, or no kids things happen! More specifically it is not uncommon for people to have situations where scrtaches, screw holes, scrapes, damages and sometimes even huge holes end up on your walls. No matter the situation having the basic skill of being able to patch that up on your own can be very beneficial.

Tools/Material Required

  • drywall
  • mesh tape
  • joint compound
  • backer
  • 150 grit sanding block
  • drill
  • mud pan (optional)
  • drywall screws
  • tape measure
  • drywall saw
  • framing square
  • pencil
  • 6 or 12 inch drywall knife
  • utility knife

Instructions

1.Cut piece: Measure past the large hole at least two inches each way. Take that measurement and transfer it to the drywall piece using the pencil and straight edge. Score along the lines using the utility knife. Bend along the score to seperate your drywall piece.

2.Prepare hole: Take the drywall piece, place over large hole and outline. Once outlined use the utitly knife to score and the serated drywall knife to cut along the line. Once cut remove the remaining drywall.

3.Attatch backer: If there is no studs available cut a plywood backer. This should be a few inches longer the the width of the hole. Place the back into the hole along the long sides of the drywall edge, half exposed half covered by the drywall. Secure the backer by screwing through the drywall to the backer. Repeat this on the bottom backer.

4.Attach new drywall: Place the drywall piece that was cut for this spot. Ensure there is not more than a 1/8th inch gap around the edges. Secure drywall to backing with the screws.

5.Mesh tape: Attached the adhesive mesh tape along the seam, placing it in the center of the seam. Make sure to line it flat with no wrinkels or twists.

6.Mud Sand Repeat: Using your 6-inch mud knife apply a thin layer of joint compund over the drywall tape. After it is dry lightly sand surface. Then apply another thin coat of mud extending two inches past the first coat. Then sand or sponge smooth. If nessesary repeat with a third coat.

7.Finishing Touches: Optionaly depending where this hole is at you may need to add texture, prime and paint!
