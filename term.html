Drywall Terms

This page will allow you to get familar with common words that are used when it comes to drywall. Click the keyword below to view an image of the item.

Drywall:

Prefabricated sheets of compressed gypsum material sandwiched between face paper and backing paper. The main material for building walls in. Used in homes and commercial buildings. Also know as sheetrock.

Drywall Knife:

A wide blade for spreading joint compound. It can be used to spread mud over nail, screw indents, seams, corners etc. Comes in ranging size from 2"-12".

Joint Tape:

Paper or mesh material used to seal the joints between sheets of drywall. Joint tape is applied to the joints with joint compound.

Joint Compound:

A pre-mixed gypsum compound used for taping to cover joints and to cover fasteners (screws, nails, etc.). Also referred to as mud, spackle, all-purpose compound.

Corner Bead:

A strip of angled material used on outside corners to give them a clean, sharp appearance. a preformed angle bead that covers the ruff cut drywall corners to make a smooth sharp finished corner.

Sanding Block:

Handheld tools that can hold sand paper in order to smooth the surface of a dry coat of mud. There are many different kinds and some people even prefer to use a sanding sponge instead.

Stud:

Framing material (metal or wood) that forms a wall. Usually placed every 16 inches and is used to attach the drywall to the wall.
